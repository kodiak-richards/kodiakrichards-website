Coaching Programs

Three areas of focus.

Coaching / Wired Well

Nervous System

For people whose body is carrying more than their mind knows what to do with — chronic stress, anxiety, burnout. We work on it from two directions: top-down through how you think, and bottom-up through the body.

Nervous system regulation Anxiety Burnout Somatic
  • 12-week minimum
  • Weekly 55-minute 1:1 calls
  • Async support between sessions
  • Virtual or in person (San Diego)

The mind and body are a two-way street. You can work from the top down — how you think, the story you're running — and from the bottom up, through the body itself. Most work only goes one direction. This does both, drawing on neuroscience, physiology, and somatic practice. Regulation you build into your body and brain, not just understand.

Coaching / Connection

Relationships

The way you connect is a pattern you learned — in how you handle closeness, conflict, and needs. This is work on your side of the dynamic, whether you're changing something stuck or deepening something good.

Relationship coaching Communication Attachment Boundaries
  • 12-week minimum
  • Weekly 55-minute 1:1 calls
  • Async support between sessions
  • Virtual or in person (San Diego)

Connection isn't a soft skill — it's the thing the rest of your life runs on. How you handle closeness, conflict, and needs was set before you had a say in it, and it runs on autopilot until you can see it clearly. This is one-on-one work — your side of the dynamic, not couples therapy — looking at the patterns underneath. Draws on attachment theory, IFS, and emotionally focused work.

Coaching / Digital Wellness

Digital Wellness

For people who can see the life they want and feel their devices eating the attention it would take to build it. We look at what's underneath the pull — not just the screen time.

Digital wellness Technology addiction Screen time Focus & presence
  • 12-week minimum
  • Weekly 55-minute 1:1 calls
  • Async support between sessions
  • Virtual or in person (San Diego)

Compulsive tech use is rarely about the tech. The scroll is usually doing a job — managing stress, filling a gap in connection, quieting a question about meaning you haven't had time to sit with. Go after the screen time directly and it comes back. Go after what it's regulating and it loosens on its own. This is attention and identity work: getting your focus back for the things you actually want to spend it on. Drawing on behavioral science, neuropsychology, and the research on how these tools are built to hold you.

Therapy & Coaching

About therapy & coaching.

Coaching is collaborative, forward-focused work, for people who feel mostly well but stuck. Burnout, a life or career transition, a creative block, a relationship with technology that's gotten out of hand, or a goal you keep circling but can't quite reach. It's about clarifying where you want to go and building the habits and systems to get there. Coaching is open to anyone, anywhere.

Counseling goes where coaching can't: trauma, complex anxiety, clinical depression, grief, and longer-arc psychological growth. If you've done coaching or therapy before and still felt like something was left on the table, this is probably what you're looking for. Therapy is available to California residents only.

Therapy and coaching run the same rhythm: sessions just under an hour (around 53 minutes), usually weekly or biweekly depending on your goals and schedule. Both are offered virtually or in person in San Diego, though in-person slots are limited and can't always be guaranteed.

Therapy is provided under licensed clinical supervision, using an integrative, person-centered approach that draws on IFS, somatic work, attachment theory, and plain, honest conversation. Because coaching involves no clinical diagnosis or treatment, it's open beyond California; therapy is for California residents only.

Both start with a free discovery call, where we get clear on what you need and whether we're a good fit.

Think of your inner world like a garden. Therapy is the weeding: healing, processing the past, and tending the wounds that crowd out new growth. Processing trauma in particular lives here, not in coaching. Coaching is the seeding: planting new habits, clarifying your vision, and moving toward what you want to build.

They're not interchangeable, and I won't pretend otherwise. If you're not sure which one fits, we'll sort it out together on the discovery call.
